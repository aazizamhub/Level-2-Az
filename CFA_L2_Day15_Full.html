SECTION 1

Pendahuluan — Framework Parity Conditions

Dalam ekonomi internasional, investor, perusahaan, dan bank sentral selalu menghadapi pertanyaan fundamental: berapa nilai tukar yang "benar" untuk suatu pasangan valuta? Apakah rupiah undervalued atau overvalued terhadap dolar? Apakah yen seharusnya lebih kuat atau lebih lemah?

Parity conditions (kondisi keseimbangan) adalah framework yang menjelaskan bagaimana berbagai pasar finansial dan barang — pasar mata uang, pasar obligasi, pasar saham, dan perdagangan barang — saling terkait. Ketika semua kondisi ini dipenuhi secara simultan, pasar dikatakan dalam equilibrium.

Ada lima parity conditions utama yang akan kita pelajari:

  1. Covered Interest Rate Parity (CIP) — hubungan antara spot rate, forward rate, dan suku bunga domestik vs foreign.
  2. Uncovered Interest Rate Parity (UIP) — hubungan antara suku bunga, ekspektasi perubahan spot rate, dan return investasi.
  3. Forward Rate Parity — forward rate sebagai predictor tidak bias (unbiased) dari future spot rate.
  4. Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) — hubungan antara price levels dan nilai tukar.
  5. International Fisher Effect — hubungan antara nominal interest rates, real rates, dan expected inflation.

Masing-masing kondisi didasarkan pada no-arbitrage principle (tidak ada peluang untung tanpa risiko). Ketika arbitrage opportunities muncul, trader akan memanfaatkannya hingga pasar kembali ke equilibrium.

Kunci Pembelajaran

Parity conditions bukanlah law of nature — tetapi konsekuensi logis dari arbitrage dan ekspektasi rasional. Ketika parity conditions meleset dalam realitas, itu memberikan peluang atau indikasi risk premium, behavioral factors, atau market inefficiencies.

SECTION 2

Covered Interest Rate Parity (CIP)

Konsep Dasar dan No-Arbitrage Condition

Covered Interest Rate Parity (CIP) menjelaskan hubungan no-arbitrage antara spot rate, forward rate, dan perbedaan suku bunga antar negara. "Covered" berarti risiko valuta asing sudah dikurangi (hedged) melalui forward contract.

Bayangkan Anda seorang investor di Indonesia dengan dana dalam rupiah. Anda memiliki dua strategi untuk berinvestasi dalam aset berdenominasi dolar:

  • Strategi A (Domestic): Investasikan rupiah dalam obligasi Indonesia dengan suku bunga id = 6% selama 1 tahun.
  • Strategi B (Foreign, Hedged): Konversi rupiah ke dolar dengan spot rate S(USD/IDR), investasikan dolar dalam obligasi AS dengan suku bunga if = 4%, dan lakukan forward contract untuk mengkonversi kembali ke rupiah.

Dalam equilibrium, return dari kedua strategi harus sama (setelah dikurangi biaya transaksi). Jika tidak, ada arbitrage opportunity.

Formula CIP

CIP Equation (Exact Form) Ff/d = Sf/d × (1 + if) / (1 + id) Dimana:
Ff/d = Forward rate (foreign per domestic)
Sf/d = Spot rate (foreign per domestic)
if = Foreign interest rate
id = Domestic interest rate

Atau dalam bentuk simplified:
(Ff/d - Sf/d) / Sf/d ≈ if - id

Interpretasi: Forward premium/discount ≈ interest rate differential

Perhatian khusus: jika if > id, maka F > S, artinya foreign currency bersuku bunga tinggi akan terdepresiasi (forward premium). Ini masuk akal: jika AS menawarkan bunga lebih tinggi, itu karena pasar mengantisipasi dolar akan lemah di masa depan.

Arbitrage Example: CIP Violation

Exhibit 1: Covered Interest Arbitrage

Data:

  • Spot rate S($/IDR) = 0.000067 (atau IDR/$ = 15,000)
  • 1-year Forward rate F($/IDR) = 0.000069 (atau IDR/$ = 14,500, dollar lebih kuat forward)
  • IDR interest rate = 6%
  • USD interest rate = 3%

Test CIP:

CIP implies: F/S ≈ (1 + 0.03) / (1 + 0.06) = 1.03 / 1.06 = 0.9717

Actual F/S = 0.000069 / 0.000067 = 1.0299

CIP VIOLATED! Forward dollar is too strong.

Arbitrage Strategy (IDR-based investor):

  1. Borrow 15,000,000 IDR @ 6% = repay 15,900,000 IDR
  2. Convert @ spot: 15,000,000 IDR ÷ 15,000 = $1,000
  3. Lend $ @ 3% = receive $1,030
  4. Lock forward: convert $1,030 @ 14,500 = 14,935,000 IDR
  5. Repay IDR loan: -15,900,000 IDR
  6. Profit: -965,000 IDR locked in (wait, recalculate...)

Sebenarnya, coba dari perspektif $ borrower: Pinjam $ @ 3%, convert ke IDR @ 15,000 per dollar, pinjam di Indo...ini semakin kompleks. Intuitifnya, jika forward terlalu mahal (dollar terlalu kuat), seseorang akan short dollar forward dan borrow dollar.

Key Assumptions of CIP

  • 1
    Zero transaction costs: Tidak ada bid-ask spread, tidak ada komisi. Dalam realitas, spread kecil membuat CIP menjadi range, bukan exact equality.
  • 2
    Same maturity and liquidity: Obligasi domestic dan foreign harus memiliki maturity yang sama dan risk level yang sama (default risk diasumsikan identik).
  • 3
    No capital controls: Dana dapat bebas bergerak antar negara. Capital controls membuat arbitrage tidak possible.
  • 4
    No default risk differential: Pasar mengasumsikan obligasi government AS dan Indonesia memiliki risiko default yang sama (obviously false, tapi asumsi ini untuk isolate FX effects).
Empirical Observation

CIP adalah salah satu parity conditions yang paling kuat secara empiris dan paling sering terpenuhi di pasar finansial modern. Ini karena covered arbitrage mudah dilaksanakan oleh institutional investors dengan akses ke forward markets dan funding markets yang deep.

Knowledge Check 1: CIP Calculation ▼

Diberikan: S(€/USD) = 1.10, iUSD = 2%, i€ = 1%, maturity 1 tahun. Berapa forward rate (€/USD) menurut CIP?

Jawaban:

F = S × (1 + i€) / (1 + iUSD)

F = 1.10 × (1.01) / (1.02) = 1.10 × 0.99020 = 1.0892

Interpretasi: Euro mengalami forward premium (F < S) karena suku bunga euro lebih rendah. Euro akan appreciate sedikit dalam forward market untuk menyeimbangkan return.

SECTION 3

Uncovered Interest Rate Parity (UIP) & Forward Rate Parity

Uncovered Interest Rate Parity (UIP)

Jika CIP adalah kondisi no-arbitrage untuk investor yang hedge FX risk, maka UIP adalah kondisi yang diterapkan untuk investor yang tidak hedge. UIP mengatakan bahwa return dari investasi unhedged di foreign country harus sama dengan return domestic, dengan perbedaannya tercermin dalam expected appreciation/depreciation of the currency.

Dengan kata lain, jika Anda berinvestasi di negara dengan suku bunga lebih tinggi tanpa hedge, Anda mengharapkan currency appreciation (negara dengan bunga tinggi) yang akan mengkompensasi bunga rendah di negara home.

Uncovered Interest Rate Parity E[%ΔSf/d] = if - id Dimana:
E[%ΔS] = Expected percentage change in spot rate = (E[Snext] - Stoday) / Stoday
if = Foreign interest rate
id = Domestic interest rate

Atau dengan rate basis:
E[Sef/d] = Sf/d × (1 + if) / (1 + id)

Intuitively: Return at home = Return abroad
(1 + id) = (1 + if) × (1 + E[%ΔSf/d])

Why Uncovered? Risk Neutral Expectation vs. Risk Premium

UIP diasumsikan berlaku ketika investors adalah risk-neutral dan memiliki expectation yang rational tentang future spot rate. Tidak ada arbitrage dalam sense ekonomi, karena Anda exposed terhadap FX risk.

Namun, dalam praktik, UIP sering tidak terpenuhi dalam jangka pendek dan menengah. Ada beberapa alasan:

  • Risk Premium: Investors menuntut additional return untuk bearing currency risk. Negara dengan volatilitas tinggi (emerging markets) menawarkan bunga tinggi, tetapi currency depreciation tidak selalu sebesar IRP memprediksi.
  • Forward Bias: Forward rate cenderung over-predict depreciation dari currency overvalued. Disebut "forward premium puzzle."
  • Behavioral Factors: Momentum trading, trend-chasing, dan sentiment dapat mendorong currency ke arah yang bertentangan dengan UIP.

Forward Rate Parity (FRP)

Forward Rate Parity adalah proposal bahwa forward rate adalah unbiased predictor dari future spot rate:

Forward Rate Parity Ff/d = E[Sef/d] Forward rate hari ini = Expected spot rate di masa depan (dalam risk-neutral expectation)

Jika kedua CIP dan UIP berlaku:
F/S = (1 + if) / (1 + id)
E[Se]/S = (1 + if) / (1 + id

Jadi F = E[Se]

FRP implikasi: forward premium/(discount) mencerminkan expected depreciation/(appreciation) dari currency.

Empirical Reality: Forward Bias

Research menunjukkan forward rate adalah biased predictor dari future spot rate, khususnya untuk high-interest-rate currencies. Ini dikenal sebagai "forward premium anomaly" atau "uncovered interest rate parity puzzle." High-yield currencies tidak depreciate seperti diprediksi UIP, menciptakan profitabel trading strategies (carry trade).

Knowledge Check 2: UIP vs. Realized Returns ▼

Seorang investor USD-based, iUSD = 2%, iJPY = 0%, spot S(¥/USD) = 110. Investor membeli 1-year JGB (Japanese Government Bond) unhedged.

Menurut UIP, berapa expected spot rate di akhir tahun? Jika actual spot di akhir tahun adalah 105 ¥/USD, apakah investor mendapat keuntungan?

Jawaban:

UIP prediction:

E[%ΔS] = iJPY - iUSD = 0% - 2% = -2%

E[S] = 110 × (1 - 0.02) = 107.8 ¥/USD

Actual spot: 105 ¥/USD (yen lebih kuat dari UIP prediction)

Return calculation:

JGB return: 0% (Japan zero rates)

FX gain: (107.8 - 105) / 105 = 2.67%

Total return: ~2.67% (keuntungan, karena yen appreciate lebih dari expected UIP)

Insight: Yen appreciated lebih dari diprediksi UIP, sehingga investor USD mendapat keuntungan tambahan dari carry trade positif.

SECTION 4

Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)

Law of One Price and Absolute PPP

Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) adalah framework yang berpendapat bahwa nilai tukar seharusnya membuat harga barang dan jasa sama di seluruh negara (setelah adjustment untuk exchange rate). Ini berasal dari Law of One Price: barang identik di pasar yang berbeda harus mempunyai harga yang sama (dalam mata uang yang sama).

Misalnya, jika sebuah Big Mac dijual seharga $5 di Amerika Serikat dan Rp75,000 di Indonesia, maka spot rate seharusnya: 75,000 / 5 = 15,000 IDR/USD. Jika spot rate actual adalah 16,000, maka Big Mac lebih murah di Indonesia (arbitrage opportunity!).

Law of One Price (for a single good) Pfx = Sf/d × Pdx Pfx = Price of good x in foreign country
Pdx = Price of same good in domestic country
Sf/d = Spot exchange rate (foreign units per domestic unit)

Rearranged: Sf/d = Pfx / Pdx

Jika law of one price berlaku untuk semua barang dan jasa, maka dapat diagregasi untuk price levels keseluruhan:

Absolute PPP Sf/d = Pf / Pd S = Spot exchange rate
Pf = Price level in foreign country
Pd = Price level in domestic country

Interpretation: The exchange rate equals the ratio of price levels.

Relative PPP (Ex-Post)

Absolute PPP jarang terpenuhi karena harga barang berbeda secara struktural antar negara (tastes, taxes, transport costs, non-tradeable goods). Namun, versi yang lebih lemah — Relative PPP — sering lebih berguna: perubahan spot rate seharusnya mencerminkan perbedaan inflasi antar negara.

Relative PPP (Ex-Post) %ΔSf/d = πf - πd %ΔS = Percentage change in spot rate over period
πf = Inflation rate in foreign country
πd = Inflation rate in domestic country

Atau dalam level form:
St / St-1 = (1 + πf) / (1 + πd)

Logic: Jika inflation lebih tinggi di luar negeri, currency mereka akan depreciate untuk maintain purchasing power parity.

Ex-Ante PPP (Expected Inflation)

Untuk forward-looking investors, ex-ante PPP menggunakan expected inflation:

Ex-Ante PPP E[%ΔSf/d] = πfe - πde E[%ΔS] = Expected percentage change in spot
πe = Expected inflation

This links PPP to UIP: jika both PPP dan UIP berlaku, maka suku bunga diferential ≈ expected inflation differential.

Why PPP Often Fails

Exhibit 2: PPP Violations — Real Examples
Reason for PPP Failure Example
Non-tradeable goods: Services (haircuts, rent) tidak bisa diarbitrase Haircut 10x lebih mahal di Zurich vs. Jakarta, tapi tidak bisa dibeli di Jakarta lalu dijual di Zurich
Trade barriers: Tariffs, quotas menghambat arbitrage Proteksi agricultural products membuat harga berbeda secara signifikan
Transport costs: Arbitrage tidak profitable jika shipping mahal Barang berat dengan margin kecil (batu) tidak diarbitrase globally
Sampling issues: Different consumption baskets CPI Indonesia vs. CPI US mengandung barang yang berbeda dengan weights berbeda
Real exchange rate shocks: Preference shifts, productivity changes Appreciasi dolar akibat safe-haven demand atau high productivity growth, bukan inflation

Empirical evidence menunjukkan bahwa:

  • Short-term (0-2 years): PPP works very poorly. Real exchange rates dapat berfluktuasi 20-30% tanpa revert cepat ke PPP level.
  • Medium-term (2-5 years): PPP starts working better, dengan half-life of deviation sekitar 3-5 years.
  • Long-term (5+ years): PPP works reasonably well untuk major currencies. Over very long horizons (20+ years), PPP holds much better.
Mean Reversion and the Real Exchange Rate

Meskipun PPP tidak berlaku sempurna, real exchange rate (S × Pd / Pf) menunjukkan mean-reversion tendency dalam jangka panjang. Ini berarti jika suatu currency overvalued (real terms), kemungkinan akan melemah di masa depan untuk mengembalikan keseimbangan.

Knowledge Check 3: PPP Calculation ▼

Inflasi Indonesia (πIDR) = 3.5%, Inflasi USA (πUSD) = 2%. Spot rate saat ini S(IDR/USD) = 15,000. Menurut relative PPP, berapa expected spot rate tahun depan?

Jawaban:

Relative PPP: %ΔS = πIDR - πUSD = 3.5% - 2% = 1.5%

Expected Snext year = 15,000 × (1 + 0.015) = 15,225 IDR/USD

Interpretasi: Rupiah diharapkan depreciate 1.5% terhadap dolar, karena inflasi Indonesia lebih tinggi dari USA. Dengan kata lain, spot rate akan bergerak dari 15,000 menjadi 15,225 (lebih banyak rupiah per dolar), mencerminkan purchasing power loss dari rupiah.

SECTION 5

Fisher Effect & Keterkaitan Seluruh Parity Conditions

Domestic Fisher Effect

Fisher Effect mengatakan bahwa nominal interest rate adalah sum dari real interest rate dan expected inflation:

Domestic Fisher Effect (Fisher Equation) i = r + πe i = Nominal interest rate
r = Real interest rate
πe = Expected inflation

Intuition: Jika Anda meminjam/meminjamkan uang, Anda menginginkan return real (after inflation). Nominal rate disesuaikan untuk expected inflation.

Contoh: Jika real rate = 2% dan expected inflation = 3%, maka nominal rate harus 5% (approximately 2% + 3%; exactly (1.02 × 1.03 - 1) = 5.06%).

International Fisher Effect

Jika Fisher Effect berlaku di kedua negara, maka:

International Fisher Effect if - id = πfe - πde Nominal interest rate differential = Expected inflation differential

id = rd + πde
if = rf + πfe

Jika rd = rf (real rates converge), maka:
if - id = πfe - πde

The Complete Parity System

Ketika semua parity conditions berlaku secara simultan, ada elegant relationship:

Integration of All Parity Conditions Jika CIP, UIP, ex-ante PPP, dan International Fisher berlaku:

(F - S) / S = if - id = πfe - πde = E[%ΔS]

Dan:
F = E[Se]
rd ≈ rf (real rates converge) This integrated view shows:
1. Forward premium reflects expected currency depreciation (UIP)
2. Expected depreciation reflects inflation differentials (ex-ante PPP)
3. Interest differentials equal inflation differentials (International Fisher)
4. Forward rate unbiased predictor of spot (FRP)
5. Real rates equalize across countries (IRP)

Illustrative Example: Complete System

Exhibit 3: Parity System Integration — IDR vs USD
Variable IDR USD Differential
Expected inflation 4% 2% 2%
Real interest rate 2% 2% 0%
Nominal interest rate (i = r + πe) 6% 4% 2%
Spot rate 15,000 IDR/USD —
Forward premium (from CIP) (F - S)/S ≈ 6% - 4% = 2% —
Forward rate: F = S × (1.04)/(1.06) 15,000 × 0.9811 = 14,717 IDR/USD —
Expected depreciation of IDR (from UIP) E[%ΔS] ≈ 2% (Rupiah diharapkan depreciate) —
Expected spot next year 15,000 × 1.02 = 15,300 IDR/USD —

Key Observations:

  • Interest differential (2%) = Inflation differential (2%): International Fisher Effect holds.
  • Forward premium (2%) ≈ Expected depreciation (2%): UIP and FRP hold.
  • Real rates are equal (2% in both countries): International Parity of real rates holds.
  • Forward rate ≠ spot rate: Forward premium indicates rupiah akan lemah, reflecting higher expected inflation dan higher nominal rates di Indonesia.

Sistem ini elegant tetapi memiliki asumsi kuat: risk neutrality, rational expectations, perfect capital mobility, zero transaction costs. Dalam kenyataan, sistem ini sering meleset, menciptakan arbitrage opportunities dan trading strategies seperti carry trade.

Knowledge Check 4: Parity System Integration ▼

Given: Real rate global = 1.5%. Forecast inflation: Brazil 6%, USA 2%. Spot S(BRL/USD) = 5.0. Berapa nominal rates dan forward rate menurut parity conditions?

Jawaban:

Step 1: Nominal rates dari Fisher Effect

iBRL = 1.5% + 6% = 7.5%

iUSD = 1.5% + 2% = 3.5%

Step 2: Forward rate dari CIP

F = S × (1 + iUSD) / (1 + iBRL) = 5.0 × 1.035 / 1.075 = 5.0 × 0.9627 = 4.814 BRL/USD

Step 3: Verificasi UIP

Expected depreciation = iBRL - iUSD = 7.5% - 3.5% = 4%

Expected spot: 5.0 × 1.04 = 5.20 BRL/USD (matches forward per FRP)

Catatan: Forward rate < spot karena BRL has higher nominal rate; real rate same, jadi perbedaan purely dari inflation differential.

SECTION 6

The Carry Trade — Mekanisme, Risiko, dan Unwind

What is the Carry Trade?

Carry trade adalah strategi investasi yang memanfaatkan UIP violations. Investasi membeli (long) high-yield currencies dan (short) low-yield currencies, dengan target mengumpulkan interest rate differential tanpa terbuka terhadap depreciation dari high-yield currency.

Menurut UIP, Anda seharusnya indifferent antara holding low-yield dan high-yield assets (karena high-yield currency akan depreciate sebesar IRP). Tetapi dalam praktek, high-yield currencies sering tidak depreciate seperti diprediksi. Ini creates "free lunch"—earn interest differential tanpa matching depreciation.

Contoh: Pada 2005-2007, JPY yields ~0% sedangkan AUD (Australian Dollar) yields ~5-6%. Carry traders meminjam JPY, convert ke AUD, invest di Australian bonds. Mereka earn 5-6% per tahun dari interest, plus AUD appreciation (karena AUD tidak depreciate seperti UIP memprediksi). This was carry trade in full bloom, sampai 2008 financial crisis.

Mechanics of the Carry Trade

Exhibit 4: Step-by-step Carry Trade Execution

Setup: JPY 0% funding currency, BRL 10% asset currency

  1. Borrow JPY: Borrow ¥1,000,000,000 @ 0% interest (overnight rates)
  2. Spot conversion: Convert ¥1B @ spot 200 ¥/BRL = BRL 5,000,000
  3. Invest in BRL: Buy Brazilian government bond yielding 10% = earn BRL 500,000 per year
  4. Roll funding: Each day/week, renew JPY borrowing @ ~0% (cost: nearly zero)
  5. Unwind (exit): Sell BRL bonds, convert proceeds back to JPY @ new spot rate, repay JPY loan

Annual Return Analysis (Unhedged):

  • Interest carry: +10% (BRL bond return)
  • FX impact: Depends on whether BRL appreciates or depreciates
  • UIP prediction: BRL should depreciate 10% (to offset interest differential)
  • Actual (if BRL flat or appreciates): Carry trader profits 10%+ FX gain

Return Decomposition

Return dari unhedged carry trade dapat decompose:

Carry Trade Return (Unhedged, Base Currency = Low Yield) Rcarry ≈ iasset - ifunding + %ΔSasset/funding Rcarry = Total return dalam funding currency
iasset = Interest rate di asset country
ifunding = Interest rate di funding country
%ΔS = Percentage change in asset currency (positive = appreciation)

Example:
If iBRL = 10%, iJPY = 0%, %ΔBRL/JPY = +5%:
R = 10% - 0% + 5% = 15% (excellent!)

If %ΔBRL/JPY = -10% (BRL depreciates):
R = 10% - 0% - 10% = 0% (carry trade hedged against depreciation per UIP)

Risk Profile of Carry Trade

Meskipun carry trade terlihat seperti "free lunch," ada risiko signifikan:

Key Risks in Carry Trade

1. Currency Risk (Tail Risk): Ketika risk sentiment berubah, high-yield currencies dapat crash secara sudden. Ini disebut "carry trade unwind." Return distribution adalah negatively skewed—banyak kecil gains tapi occasional large losses.

2. Interest Rate Risk: Central bank dapat raise rates (mengganggu funding rates) atau terjadi repricing aset (bond values drop). Funding costs bisa meningkat dramatically.

3. Leverage Risk: Carry trades sering dijalankan dengan leverage tinggi (borrowed capital). Leverage amplify gains dan losses.

4. Liquidity Risk: Ketika unwind terjadi, withdrawal dari carry positions dapat menciptakan fire-sale prices dan illiquidity.

5. Rollover Risk: Funding rates dapat spike, atau lenders mungkin tidak roll over exposure, memaksa liquidation.

Empirical Return Distribution

Carry trade returns sering dideskripsikan sebagai "picking up nickels in front of steamroller" — memang earn steady small returns (nickels), tapi setiap beberapa tahun ada sudden large loss (steamroller). Distribusi return-nya:

  • Peaked: Return sering concentrate di range narrow (e.g., 5-15% per tahun)
  • Fat Tails: Probabilitas extreme losses lebih tinggi dari normal distribution
  • Negative Skewness: Tail risk ke bawah (crash losses) lebih severe daripada upside
  • Low Correlation dengan Risk Assets: Carry trade returns generally uncorrelated dengan stock returns dalam normal times, tapi saat market stress keduanya crash bersamaan (correlation spike)

Carry Trade Unwind Dynamics

Ketika carry trade unwind dimulai (seringkali triggered oleh risk-off event), ada cascade effects:

Exhibit 5: Anatomy of Carry Trade Unwind

Scenario: Risk-off Event (e.g., banking crisis, credit shock)

  1. Trigger: Sudden risk-off sentiment atau adverse economic news. Investors start covering losses.
  2. Unwinding Begins: Carry traders forced (or voluntary) to exit positions. Sell asset currency (BRL, AUD, etc.), buy funding currency (JPY, CHF, USD).
  3. Currency Impact: Asset currency crashes (high-yielders depreciate sharply), funding currency appreciates (safe-haven bid).
  4. Margin Calls: Leverage positions see significant losses. Brokers issue margin calls. Traders forced to liquidate.
  5. Stop-Loss Triggers: Technical traders' stop-losses triggered as price falls, creating additional selling pressure.
  6. Liquidity Spiral: Excessive supply of asset currency and demand for safe-haven currency worsens depreciation.
  7. Flight to Quality: Broader risk-off drives investment flows dari emerging markets dan risky assets ke safe assets (T-bills, gold).
  8. Funding Pressure: JPY borrowing rates may spike atau become unavailable. Worse, brokers may require higher collateral.
  9. Cascade Losses: Losses compound as position liquidations continue.

Historical Example: August 2015 China Devaluation

PBOC surprise devaluation of CNY plus weak data triggered risk-off. Carry trades in CNY, EMs unwound. VIX spiked, JPY (safe-haven) surged ~5% in days, commodities crashed. Losses in carry positions estimated in tens of billions.

Profitability of Carry Trade Over Time

Carry trade historically has been profitable on average, tapi dengan extreme volatility dan occasional losses. Pengalaman empiris:

  • 2003-2007: Highly profitable; average returns 8-12% annually. Low volatility, steady carry accumulation.
  • 2008 Financial Crisis: Devastating unwind; emerging market currencies crashed 20-40%. Carry traders suffered massive losses in single quarter.
  • 2009-2011: Profitable again; carry recovered as risk sentiment normalized.
  • 2011-2012: Another difficult period (Eurozone crisis) with volatility spikes.
  • 2013-2015: Mixed; US rates rising pressured emerging market funding.
  • 2020: COVID crash in March; recovery in subsequent quarters.

(a) TRY appreciates 3%:

R = 12% - 2% + 3% = 13% (best case, earn carry + FX gain)

(b) TRY depreciates 8%:

R = 12% - 2% - 8% = 2% (small profit, FX loss partially offset carry)

(c) TRY depreciates 10%:

R = 12% - 2% - 10% = 0% (break-even, FX loss exactly equals carry; this is UIP prediction!)

Insight: Scenario (c) matches UIP: interest rate differential (10%) equals depreciation (10%). Scenarios (a) dan (b) show UIP violation—carry is profitable despite depreciation.

Soal-soal berikut mengikuti format CFA Level 2 Item Set dengan satu vignette diikuti 6 pertanyaan. Tingkat kesulitan: Medium-Hard.

VIGNETTE — SCENARIO ANALYSIS

Situation:

Seorang portfolio manager dari Indonesia, PT Mitra Investasi, sedang menganalisis strategi investasi lintas valuta (IDR/USD/JPY) untuk tahun depan. Data pasar saat ini:

Variable Indonesia (IDR) USA (USD) Japan (JPY)
Spot Rate — IDR/USD = 15,000 JPY/USD = 110
1Y Nominal Interest Rate 6.5% 4.5% 0.5%
Expected Inflation (1Y) 4.0% 2.5% 0.8%
Expected Real Rate 2.5% 2.0% ~0%
1Y Forward Rate — F(IDR/USD) = 15,500 F(JPY/USD) = 109.5

Additional Context:

• PT Mitra currently holds significant IDR assets and is considering unhedged investments abroad.
• Bank Indonesia recently signaled 1-2 rate hikes over next 12 months to combat inflation.
• Analyst forecasts that global risk sentiment may deteriorate if Fed signals earlier rate peak, potentially triggering emerging market currency weakness.
• The manager is specifically considering: (1) Covered investment in USD bonds, (2) Uncovered investment in USD bonds, (3) Carry trade exposure via JPY funding.

Question 1
Based on CIP, the forward rate premium/discount for IDR/USD should most likely be:

Answer: B

CIP states: (F - S) / S ≈ iIDR - iUSD = 6.5% - 4.5% = 2%

Forward discount = 2%, meaning F < S. IDR should trade weaker (more IDR per USD) in forward market.

Calculated forward: F = 15,000 × (1.045) / (1.065) = 15,000 × 0.9812 = 14,718 IDR/USD

But given F(IDR/USD) = 15,500, forward is stronger than CIP predicts! This suggests CIP is violated, possibly due to risk premium or expectations of further BI tightening supporting IDR.

Question 2
According to UIP, if the manager invests in USD bonds unhedged, the expected return in IDR terms should approximately equal the IDR bond return (6.5%) if:

Answer: A

UIP: Expected return from unhedged USD bond = iUSD + E[%ΔS(USD/IDR)]

For returns to be equal: 6.5% = 4.5% + E[%ΔS(USD/IDR)]

E[%ΔS(USD/IDR)] = 2% (USD depreciates or IDR depreciates by 2%)

From the investor's IDR perspective, if IDR depreciates 2% against USD, USD investment returns are offset, equaling the 6.5% IDR bond return.

This matches the interest rate differential (2%) per UIP principle.

Question 3
Relative PPP suggests that the expected spot rate IDR/USD in one year should be approximately:

Answer: A

Relative PPP: Snext = Stoday × (1 + πIDR) / (1 + πUSD)

Snext = 15,000 × (1.040) / (1.025) = 15,000 × 1.01463 = 15,219 IDR/USD

Interpretation: IDR expected to depreciate ~1.46% due to higher inflation (4.0% vs 2.5%), reflecting purchasing power loss.

Note: This PPP forecast (1.5% depreciation) is less than the interest rate differential (2%), suggesting potential UIP violation and carry trade opportunity in favor of USD.

Question 4
The manager observes that the forward rate F(IDR/USD) = 15,500 is stronger (lower IDR per USD) than both the CIP-implied rate and the PPP-implied rate. This is most likely due to:

Answer: A

Forward rate (15,500) < CIP-implied (14,718) means IDR is stronger in forward. This contradicts CIP's prediction of IDR depreciation.

Given vignette context (BI signals rate hikes, stronger growth expectations), market is pricing in IDR strength due to positive policy action and capital flows. The forward premium reflects risk premium and expectation of IDR strength.

Option B is wrong because CIP/PPP always set bounds (with spreads). Option C is backwards (if long USD forward, would push dollar weaker, not stronger).

Question 5
If the manager enters a carry trade (short JPY at 0.5% borrowing, long IDR at 6.5%), the annualized return (in JPY terms) will be approximately:

Answer: B

Carry trade return: R = iIDR - iJPY + %ΔIDRvs JPY

Base carry = 6.5% - 0.5% = 6.0%

Total return = 6.0% + %ΔIDR/JPY

If IDR appreciates vs JPY, return > 6%; if depreciates, return < 6%. But base carry is guaranteed if position maintained (assuming rates don't change dramatically).

Option C incorrectly assumes PPP depreciation; while PPP suggests IDR depreciates vs USD by ~1.5%, the relationship between IDR/JPY is different.

Question 6
The analyst warns that if global risk sentiment deteriorates (e.g., Fed policy shock), the carry trade position (short JPY, long IDR) would most likely experience:

Answer: B

Risk-off environment triggers carry trade unwind. Typical dynamics:

1. JPY (funding currency, safe-haven) appreciates → short JPY position loses

2. IDR (emerging market, risky asset) depreciates → long IDR position loses

3. Losses compound because both positions move against the trader simultaneously

Key insight: Carry trades have negative skewness—small gains accumulated over time wiped out by occasional large losses during risk-off periods. This is the "steamroller" effect.

Option A is backwards (IDR depreciates in risk-off). Option C is false (interest rate differential doesn't protect against tail FX risk).
