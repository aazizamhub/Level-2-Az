Learning Objectives
By the end of this subtopic, you'll be able to:
  • Bedakan endogenous growth model dari neoclassical model — terutama peran saving rate dan diminishing returns
  • Jelaskan tiga hipotesis convergence (absolute, conditional, club) dan implikasi investasinya
  • Evaluasi argumen "resource curse" dan pengaruh natural resources terhadap pertumbuhan ekonomi
  • Terapkan analisis demografis (population growth, labor force participation, immigration) untuk memproyeksikan potential GDP growth
Key Concepts
1
Endogenous Growth Model

Gue jelasin dulu perbedaannya dari Solow. Kalau Solow (neoclassical) nganggap teknologi itu "given" (exogenous), model endogenous growth nyatain teknologi dan inovasi muncul dari dalam sistem — terutama dari R&D spending dan human capital accumulation.

Key differences dari Solow:

  • Production function LINEAR, bukan diminishing returns: ye = c·ke dimana c = constant marginal product of capital
  • Saving rate PERMANENTLY meningkatkan growth rate (bukan cuma short-term effects)
  • Nggak ada "steady-state" yang ditetapkan — ekonomi terus tumbuh
  • Private returns vs social returns distinction — ada positive externalities dari R&D

Growth rate formula:

Δye/ye = Δke/ke = sc − δ − n

dimana: s = saving rate, c = constant MPC of capital, δ = depreciation rate, n = labor force growth

Kunci: Semakin tinggi saving rate, semakin tinggi permanent growth rate. Ini beda BANGET dari Solow dimana higher saving cuma geser level, bukan slope-nya.

R&D & Positive Externalities: Gue catat — Romer (1986) model nyatain bahwa private returns dari R&D mungkin < social returns. Ada spillover effects: kalau satu firma develop teknologi, kompetitor bisa adopsi juga (partial). Ini solusi monopoly problem dalam innovation.

2
Convergence Hypotheses: Absolute, Conditional, Club

Ini penting banget untuk portfolio managers — implikasinya untuk investasi di emerging markets.

Convergence Type Definition Implication
Absolute Developing countries eventually catch up regardless of characteristics Poor countries akan otomatis richer; high growth guaranteed
Conditional Convergence conditional on same saving rate, population growth, production function Converge to same LEVEL AND RATE; perlu samakan fundamentals
Club Only rich/middle-income "club members" converge; poor countries stay behind if institutions weak Need institutional reforms to join club; high returns untuk club members

Sources of convergence (jika ada):

  • Capital deepening: Negara dengan low K/L ratio tumbuh lebih cepat (diminishing returns logic)
  • Technology adoption: Advanced countries bisa copy teknologi dari developed countries (imitation effect)
Exam Trap
Don't confuse "convergence of growth RATES" (Solow implies ini) dengan "convergence of income LEVELS" (juga implies, but takes forever). Neoclassical model predict rates converge, tapi levels perlu long-long time.

Investment implication: Investing in lower-income "club members" (negara yang stabilnya sudah bagus, institutions strong) should provide higher long-term returns daripada developed markets. Tapi countries di "periphery" tanpa institutional strength = risiko tinggi.

3
Natural Resources & the Resource Curse

Counterintuitive: negara dengan natural resources berlimpah sering UNDERPERFORM dibanding yang miskin resources.

Resource types:

  • Renewable: forests, fisheries (sustainable kalau managed well)
  • Non-renewable: oil, coal, minerals (depleting, finite)

Key insight: Ownership of natural resources is NOT necessary for high income. South Korea dan Singapore both rich tanpa significant natural resources — they built human/knowledge capital instead.

Resource Curse explained:

  • Reason 1 (Institutions): Resource-rich countries often fail to develop proper economic institutions. Government corruption, rent-seeking behavior, weak property rights — semua ini menghambat broader economic development.
  • Reason 2 (Dutch Disease): Resource exports → currency appreciation → manufacturing sector becomes uncompetitive. Contoh nyata: Indonesia, Nigeria, Venezuela. Resource money dominate, manufactures shrink.

Real examples:

Venezuela (oil-rich) vs South Korea (resource-poor): Venezuela stagnant/decline, SK developed. Nigeria (oil) underperforms vs structured economies. Ini bukan coincidence — institutional problem + Dutch disease.

Non-renewable depletion concern: Gue note — orang sering overstated resource depletion. Realitynya: (1) technology enables substitution (renewable energy for oil), (2) price mechanism encourages shifts. So nggak se-critical yang katanya.

4
Demographics & Labor Supply Drivers

Labor force growth = f(population growth, labor force participation rate, net migration, avg hours worked). Ini fundamental untuk potential GDP growth projection.

Population growth:

  • Higher di developing countries, lower di developed (Europe, Japan aging)
  • Increases TOTAL GDP growth tapi NOT per capita (unless accompanied by productivity)
  • Can be blessing (young workforce = growth potential) or curse (unemployment risk)

Age mix matters critically:

  • Aging demographic (Europe, Japan, South Korea): More retirees, fewer workers → demographic burden. Lower growth potential, pension pressure.
  • Young demographic (Indonesia, India, Nigeria): Demographic boost potential IF they get jobs and education. "Demographic dividend."

Labor force participation:

  • Rising female participation boosts growth (Spain example: LFPR naik, GDP growth acceleration)
  • But this is a ONE-TIME transition effect, not permanent
  • Once everyone participates, boost ends

Immigration impact: Net migration bisa boost labor supply, especially untuk aging economies. But political sensitivity besar.

Practical: Untuk potential GDP analysis, harus decompose growth ke (1) population growth, (2) LFPR changes, (3) productivity. Demographics sedang powerful lever untuk growth projections.
Quick Reference — Cheat Sheet
Endogenous Growth Formula
Δye/ye = sc − δ − n
Higher s → permanently higher growth (key diff from Solow)
Convergence Types
Absolute: Auto catch-up
Conditional: Same fundamentals
Club: Institutions matter
Resource Curse Causes
1) Weak institutions + rent-seeking
2) Dutch disease (currency appreciation)
Labor Force Growth Drivers
Population growth, LFPR, migration, hours worked. Demographic dividend if young + employed.
Exam Weight
~5% on Level 2. Mostly standalone questions, some integration with macro.
Key Insight
Institutions > natural resources for long-term growth. Endogenous model = innovation-driven.
Practice Questions — Indonesian Vignette (30 min)
Vignette
Budi Santoso adalah portfolio manager di Jakarta Capital, managing Rp 500 milyar untuk Indonesian institutional investors. Dia analyzing long-term growth prospects di tiga economies: Indonesia, Brazil, dan Saudi Arabia. Indonesia: Population growth 1.1% pa, LFPR increasing (female participation rising dari 48% to expected 55% over 10 years). Saving rate 32%, technology spillovers moderate. Average annual hours: 1,970. Natural resources (palm oil, coal, tin) significant but institutions improving. Brazil: Population growth 0.7% pa, LFPR stabilizing at 63%, saving rate 19%, strong manufacturing base, favorable demographics (still young). Average hours: 1,850. Saudi Arabia: Oil revenues dominant (90% exports), population growth 1.8% pa but non-Saudi labor imported, LFPR low (locals 45%, diversification plan ongoing). Saving rate 25%, natural resources massive but Dutch disease risk high. Santoso applying endogenous growth model untuk evaluate potential GDP growth. He's concerned about sustainability and long-term returns.
Question 1
Based on endogenous growth model, which economy likely show PERMANENTLY higher growth rate given its saving rate advantage?
A) Indonesia (higher saving rate 32% vs Brazil 19%)
B) Brazil (more stable demographics)
C) Saudi Arabia (oil revenues can sustain investment)
Explanation: Endogenous growth model key feature: higher saving rate PERMANENTLY increases growth rate (sc − δ − n, where s is saving rate). Indonesia's 32% vs Brazil's 19% → Indonesia gets permanent boost. In neoclassical (Solow), higher s just shifts level, not growth rate. Endogenous = totally different implication.
Question 2
Which convergence hypothesis best describes Indonesia's position, assuming strong institutional reforms continue?
A) Absolute convergence — Indonesia will automatically catch developed markets
B) Conditional convergence — institutions matter but Indonesia not yet in "club"
C) Club convergence — improving institutions allow entry to middle-income club, higher returns expected
Explanation: Indonesia has shown steady institutional improvement (transparency, markets deepening). Absolute convergence = no institutional requirement (doesn't fit here). Conditional = sama fundamentals (doesn't quite fit). Club = institutional quality is gate → Indonesia improving, joining middle-income club → expected higher returns than staying periphery. Club convergence most realistic.
Question 3
Saudi Arabia's heavy reliance on oil exports (90% of export revenue) presents which long-term growth risk?
A) Non-renewable resource depletion will force economic restructuring
B) Dutch disease (currency appreciation) will make non-oil sectors uncompetitive
C) Weak institutions are the primary barrier to growth
Explanation: Resource curse has two main mechanisms: institutions AND Dutch disease. For Saudi Arabia specifically, Dutch disease is the IMMEDIATE risk — oil export revenue → SAR appreciation → manufacturing/tourism uncompetitive. (A) is overstated per curriculum — depletion less critical than people think. (C) is true but not the mechanism described for THIS situation.
Question 4
Evaluating Indonesia's labor supply growth, which component provides a ONE-TIME boost that won't repeat?
A) Population growth rate at 1.1% pa (young population advantage)
B) Rising female LFPR (48% → 55% transition effect)
C) Immigration boosting total workforce
Explanation: Female LFPR rising is a one-time transition boost — once women are equally participating, the GROWTH from this effect stops (even if LFPR stays high). Population growth (A) continues. Immigration (C) could be ongoing. Key insight: structural labor force changes (like women entering workforce) are temporary growth boosters.
